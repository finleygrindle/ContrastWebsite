This graphic serves as a well-informed display of contrast while maintaining simplicity and a limited colour palette. The composition utilises visual tension by focusing the viewers' attention on the subject – the large type that spells “contrast”. The use of foreground and background is utilised to spell a word in an unconventional manner, yet still clearly legible to the viewer. This is a form of contrast, using darker text behind lighter text to emphasise the illusion of 3 dimensions on a 2-dimensional plane. This both enhances the depth of the composition and creates visual interest in a graphic that would otherwise be rather mundane.

A contrast in type size is also used to further the idea of tension, with smaller type being used to convey a simplistic message about typography that the viewer would then read after viewing the main subject of the graphic. The effective use of minimalism preserves easy viewing; if all elements contrast the composition can become overwhelming.
